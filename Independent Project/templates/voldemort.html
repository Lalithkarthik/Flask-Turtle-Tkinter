Voldemort's History

Voldemort, also known as Lord Voldemort, is a prominent character in the Harry Potter series created by J.K. Rowling. He is depicted as a powerful and dark wizard who seeks to gain immortality and control over the wizarding world.

History of Voldemort

Early Life: Voldemort, whose birth name is Tom Marvolo Riddle, was born on December 31, 1926, in an orphanage in London. His mother was a witch and a descendant of Salazar Slytherin, one of the founders of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

Hogwarts Years: Tom Riddle attended Hogwarts and was sorted into the Slytherin house. He was known for his exceptional magical abilities and his obsession with power. During his time at Hogwarts, he delved into dark magic and began creating Horcruxes, objects that contained pieces of his soul.

Rise to Power: After leaving Hogwarts, Voldemort started gathering followers and formed a group called the Death Eaters. He sought to establish a pure-blood wizarding society and eliminate those he considered "impure" or "mudbloods". Voldemort's reign of terror began, and he became known as the Dark Lord.

First Defeat: Voldemort's power grew, and he targeted the Potters, a family that stood against him. On October 31, 1981, he attempted to kill Harry Potter, a baby with a prophecy that foretold his downfall. However, his curse backfired, leaving him weakened and without a physical body.

Return to Power: For the next decade, Voldemort existed in a weakened and spectral form, seeking ways to regain his strength and return to power. He manipulated his loyal followers and orchestrated events to achieve his goals.

Second Rise to Power: In the fourth book, "Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire," Voldemort successfully regained his physical form and resumed his campaign of terror. He waged war against the wizarding world, leading to the Battle of Hogwarts, a climactic confrontation between his forces and those loyal to Harry Potter.

Final Defeat: In the end, Voldemort's quest for immortality and power proved to be his downfall. Harry Potter, along with his friends and allies, fought against him, ultimately leading to Voldemort's defeat in the Battle of Hogwarts on May 2, 1998.
