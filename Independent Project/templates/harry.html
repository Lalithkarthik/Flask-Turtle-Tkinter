Harry's Quidditch things

In the Harry Potter series, Harry Potter comes into possession of several Quidditch-related items. Nimbus 2000: Harry receives a Nimbus 2000 as a gift from Professor McGonagall in "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (or "Sorcerer's Stone" in the US edition) after becoming the youngest Seeker in a century for the Gryffindor Quidditch team. Firebolt: Harry receives a Firebolt as an anonymous gift in "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban". It is considered to be one of the fastest broomsticks in the wizarding world.

In addition to the Quidditch brooms, Harry Potter also owns Quidditch-related accessories, including a servicing kit and books. Servicing Kit: Harry was gifted a Quidditch servicing kit by Hermione Granger in "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince". This kit included various tools and materials for maintaining and repairing Quidditch equipment, emphasizing the importance of proper care for the broomsticks. Quidditch Books: Throughout the series, Harry is depicted reading various books related to Quidditch. These books include rulebooks, strategy guides, and historical accounts of famous Quidditch matches. While he doesn't necessarily own all these books, they are essential to his understanding and improvement as a Quidditch player. These items, along with the brooms, contribute to Harry's involvement in the sport of Quidditch and his passion for flying and competing as a Seeker for the Gryffindor Quidditch team.

In the Harry Potter series, there are several Quidditch-related books that are mentioned or play a role in Harry Potter's experiences with the sport. These books provide insight into the rules, strategies, and history of Quidditch. Quidditch Through the Ages: This book, by Kennilworthy Whisp, is a comprehensive guide to the history and rules of Quidditch. It is one of the textbooks used at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and is also a popular book among Quidditch enthusiasts. The Standard Book of Spells, Grade 1: While not specifically a Quidditch book, this textbook by Miranda Goshawk contains spells that are useful for Quidditch, such as the Levitation Charm, which could be handy for aspiring Quidditch players. The Beaters' Bible: This book is mentioned as a guide for Quidditch Beaters, who are responsible for protecting their team from the Bludgers. It likely contains strategies, techniques, and training tips for Beaters. Quidditch Teams of Britain and Ireland: This book details the history and performance of various Quidditch teams in Britain and Ireland. It provides insight into the strengths and weaknesses of different teams and their players.

These books, among others, contribute to the rich lore and culture surrounding the wizarding sport of Quidditch, offering readers and characters a deeper understanding of the game and its intricacies. Also, Ron Weasley gifts Harry Potter a book titled "Flying with the Cannons" as a birthday present. This book is about the Chudley Cannons, Ron's favorite Quidditch team. The Chudley Cannon shave a devoted fanbase despite their poor performance in the league, and Ron's enthusiasm for the team is evident throughout the series. The book "Flying with the Cannons" likely contains information about the history, players, and memorable matches of the Chudley Cannons, allowing Harry to learn more about Ron's favorite team.
