Voldemort's Horcruxes

In the Harry Potter franchise, a Horcrux is an object in which a Dark wizard or witch has hidden a detached fragment of their soul in order to become immortal. Lord Voldemort, the main antagonist, created a total of seven Horcruxes.

The Horcruxes

Tom Riddle's Diary: During his time at Hogwarts, Tom Riddlediscovered the process of creating a Horcrux through his research on dark magic. He used his own diary as the first Horcrux. He enchanted the diary with a piece of his soul, ensuring that a part of him would remain in the mortal world even if his body was destroyed. The diary was then used to possess and control Ginny Weasley, causing her to open the Chamber of Secrets.

Marvolo Gaunt's Ring: Voldemort discovered that his family's heirloom, the ring of Marvolo Gaunt, possessed powerful magical properties. He used a curse to hide the Horcrux and protect it from being discovered. In order to create the Horcrux, he committed a murder, which is considered an act of supreme evil in the wizarding world.

Salazar Slytherin's Locket: After stealing the locket, which belonged to Salazar Slytherin, Voldemort turned it into a Horcrux. He placed powerful enchantments on it to guard it and ensure its protection. The process of creating this Horcrux involved tearing a piece of his soul through the murder and enchanting the object.

Helga Hufflepuff's Cup: Voldemort stole Helga Hufflepuff's cup from the vaults of Gringotts Bankand made it into a Horcrux. He used powerful protective spells to keep it hidden and safeguarded. The creation of this Horcrux involved committing yet another murder to split his soul and bind it to the cup.

Rowena Ravenclaw's Lost Diadem: After discovering the lost diadem of Rowena Ravenclaw, Voldemort turned it into a Horcrux. He hid it in the Room of Requirement at Hogwarts, adding layers of protective enchantments. The process of creating this Horcrux required him to commit another murder, further fragmenting his soul.

Nagini the Snake: Voldemort made his pet snake, Nagini, into a Horcrux using a ritual. He performed a dark magicritual to bind a piece of his soul to her, granting her a form of immortality. This ritual involved an act of murder as well.

Harry Potter himself (unknowingly): Voldemort unintentionally made Harry Potterinto a Horcrux when he attempted to kill him as a baby. The failed killing curse rebounded, and a piece of Voldemort's shattered soul latched onto Harry, making him an unintended Horcrux.
